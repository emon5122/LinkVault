Privacy Policy

LinkVault · Last updated: 2026-07-19

LinkVault ("the app") is designed to be private by default. This policy explains what data the app handles and how.

Data we collect

None. LinkVault does not have user accounts and does not collect, transmit, or sell any personal data. We do not use analytics, advertising, or tracking SDKs.

Data stored on your device

The links you save — including their titles, URLs, descriptions, notes, folders, tags, and preview images — are stored locally on your device using an on-device database. This data never leaves your device unless you explicitly export or back it up and choose where to share the resulting file.

Network access

When you add a link, the app fetches that page's public metadata (title, description, preview image, and icon) directly from the website you saved, so it can show a preview. Opening a link loads that website in your browser. LinkVault does not send your data to us or any third party.

Permissions

  • Internet — to fetch link previews and open web pages.
  • Notifications (optional) — only if you enable reminders.

Data sharing & deletion

Because your data lives only on your device, you are in full control. Deleting a link, clearing the app's data, or uninstalling the app permanently removes it. Exports and backups are created only when you initiate them.

Children

The app does not knowingly collect any data from anyone, including children.

Changes

We may update this policy; changes will be posted on this page with a new date.
